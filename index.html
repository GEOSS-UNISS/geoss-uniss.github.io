Prof. Erica Nocerino, Dr.

Email: enocerino@uniss.it

ResearchGate: Erica Nocerino's Profile

Erica Nocerino is currently an assistant professor in geomatics at the University of Sassari, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences. Her research interests are in 3D digitization techniques, with the aim of developing procedures and guidelines for the generation of 3D models of known and verified quality in fields such as cultural heritage, archaeology, biology, and medicine. Recently, she has focused on underwater photogrammetry for autonomous navigation and image acquisition and processing for temporal monitoring. She is a scientific diver who has participated in various underwater measurement campaigns, including the Costa Concordia shipwreck and coral monitoring in French Polynesia. She serves as the chair of the ISPRS Working Group II/7 on Underwater Data Acquisition and Processing and is the PI of the ISPRS SI 2023 NAUTILUS project and the Italian project MANATEE.

Fabio Menna, Dr.

Email: fmenna@uniss.it

Fabio Menna is a researcher at the University of Sassari, Italy. His main interests include photogrammetry, range sensors, geodetic surveying, 3D modeling, and metrology. He is a scientific diver with experience in underwater photogrammetry for engineering and industrial applications, including the Costa Concordia photogrammetric survey and the development of COMEX ORUS3D subsea photogrammetry systems. He is the co-chair of the ISPRS WG II/7 on Underwater Data Acquisition and Processing. Fabio has organized several scientific events related to underwater photogrammetry, edited special issues on photogrammetry, and tutored over 15 international summer schools and tutorials. He is the PI of the ISPRS ECBI 2024 POSER project, an open-source simulation platform for teaching and training underwater photogrammetry.

Alessio Calantropio, Dr.

Email: alessio@calantropio.it

Website: Alessio Calantropio's Website

Alessio Calantropio is a postdoctoral researcher in geomatics at the University of Sassari, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences. He graduated in Architecture from Politecnico di Torino with a thesis on UAVs for construction site safety and seismic emergency response. In 2023, he obtained a Ph.D. in Geomatics, focusing on geomatic techniques for documenting underwater cultural heritage. He is a scientific diver with expertise in underwater data acquisition and processing, marine and coastal 2D and 3D mapping, and UAV applications for research, survey, humanitarian relief, public safety, and built heritage monitoring. He is a member of the ISPRS Working Group II/7 and actively contributed to the ISPRS SI 2023 NAUTILUS project for underwater geospatial studies.
